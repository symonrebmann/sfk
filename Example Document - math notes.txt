Linear Equations - Slope Intercept Form

y = mx + b
y = the output value (vertical axis)
m = slope (how steep the line is)
x = the input value (horizontal axis)
b = y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis)

To find slope between two points:
m = (y2 - y1) / (x2 - x1)

Example:
Line with slope 2 and y-intercept 3: y = 2x + 3
When x = 4: y = 2(4) + 3 = 11

Parallel lines have equal slopes.
Perpendicular lines have slopes that are negative reciprocals.
Example: slope of 2 and slope of -1/2 are perpendicular.
