Q: If a line has a slope of 2/3, what is the slope of a line perpendicular to it?
A: Id surmize the slope is -3/2
G: Correct
E: The slope of a perpendicular line is the negative reciprocal of the original line's slope.

   Work:
   Original slope = 2/3
   Perpendicular slope = -1 / (2/3) = -3/2

   Your answer of -3/2 is correct.

Q: A line is defined by the equation y = -5x + 8. What is the slope of a line that runs perpendicular to this line?
A: The slope is approximatly 1/5
G: Correct
E: In the slope-intercept form (y = mx + b), the slope is represented by m. For the given equation, the original slope is -5. The slope of a perpendicular line is its negative reciprocal.

   Work:
   Original slope = -5
   Perpendicular slope = -1 / -5 = 1/5
